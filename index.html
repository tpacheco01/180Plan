AG1 Keep 1 serving · 6am with water, empty stomach Micronutrient foundation. Adaptogens, enzymes, probiotics. Your daily baseline.
Moonbrew Keep 1 serving · 8:30–9pm, 30 min before bed Sleep is working. Protect it. Sleep quality directly drives fat loss and recovery at 60.
Fatty 15 (C15:0) Keep Per label · with dinner Promising science, not proven. Take with fat-containing meal for absorption.

Creatine Monohydrate Add 5g · post-workout shake daily #1 priority add. Preserves lean muscle in a deficit. Decades of safety data. Cheap. Also emerging cognitive benefit data.

Collagen Peptides Consider 10–15g · morning or post-workout Joint and connective tissue support at 60. Does not count toward your 150g protein target — low leucine.

Track in MacroFactor. Your 1,200 cal deficit is a target, not a certainty — most people are off by 20–30%. MacroFactor adjusts your targets weekly based on actual weight trend. Let the algorithm correct for reality.

Cottage Cheese — How to Use It Without Eating It Plain

Sweet Option
Cottage Cheese Bowl
Blend 1 cup cottage cheese smooth. Top with berries, a drizzle of honey, and crushed walnuts. Tastes like cheesecake filling. Texture completely transformed by blending.
Sweet Option
Protein Pancakes
Blend 1 cup cottage cheese + 2 bananas + ½ cup oats. Cook like pancakes. ~35g protein per batch, high satiety, genuinely good.
Savory Option
Pasta Sauce Base
Blend cottage cheese into marinara — becomes a creamy high-protein sauce. Mix into pasta or use as a pizza base. Undetectable texture, adds 20g protein per serving.
Savory Option
Dip or Spread
Blend with garlic, herbs, lemon. Use as a dip for vegetables or spread for wraps. Replaces hummus or cream cheese with 4x the protein.

Mobility & Recovery

At 60, connective tissue and joint mobility fail before cardiovascular fitness does. Ten minutes here protects everything else in the plan.

Daily Mobility Routine — 10 Minutes Post-Workout

Hip Flexors · 90 sec/side
Kneeling Lunge Stretch
Peloton cycling tightens hip flexors daily. This is your highest priority stretch. Do it every single session without exception.
Thoracic Spine · 2 min
Foam Roller T-Spine
Cycling and strength work compresses the upper back. Thoracic mobility protects your shoulders and lower back simultaneously.
Hamstrings · 60 sec/side
Standing or Lying Stretch
Tight hamstrings pull on the lower back. At your training volume, this is a slow injury waiting to happen without attention.
Shoulders · 60 sec/side
Cross-Body + Doorframe
Pressing movements and cycling posture internally rotate the shoulder. Counteract it daily.

Recovery Signals — When to Back Off

Daily weight, body fat %, muscle mass, visceral fat, vascular age. At 60 in a deficit, muscle mass matters more than scale weight. This tells the real story.

Under-mattress pad. Nothing to wear. Tracks sleep stages, overnight HR, and breathing disturbances. Flags potential apnea — common at 60 and a major hidden fat loss blocker.

Weekly Accountability Check-In — 5 Minutes Every Sunday

1. 7-day average weight (not daily — trend matters, not noise)
2. Days protein hit 150g+ (target: 5 of 7 minimum)
3. Workouts completed vs planned
4. Dumbbell weights vs prior week (holding, up, or down?)
5. One honest observation: what worked, what didn't

Keep this in Notes app or a simple document. The discipline is in the ritual, not the tool.

Travel Protocol

6 trips per year. This is where consistency breaks for most people. It won't for you if the system is already built.

Pack Always
Travel Supplement Kit
AG1 travel packs · pre-portioned creatine (5g bags) · single-serve whey packets · Moonbrew travel packs · Omega-3 caps · D3+K2 caps. All TSA compliant. Zero excuse for gaps.
Hotel Training
Bodyweight Protocol
No Peloton. No problem. 30-min bodyweight circuit: pushup variations · bodyweight squats + lunges · dips on chair · plank holds · burpees. Intensity over equipment.
Nutrition on the Road
Order Strategy
Grilled chicken or turkey at any restaurant. Lean beef option almost always exists. Greek yogurt at any hotel breakfast. Protein shake in the room before client dinners — arrive not hungry.
The Rule
Minimum Viable Day
On the worst travel day: 20 min bodyweight workout + hit protein target + take supplements. That's it. That's enough to maintain. Perfection is not the goal — continuity is.

Maintenance System

Getting to 180 is not your problem. You've said so yourself. This section is the real work. Build the landing before you need to land.

The Trigger Band — Non-Negotiable

Define this now. Commit to it before you need it.

Goal Weight
180 lbs
Acceptable Range
180–183 lbs
Trigger Point
184 lbs
Response
Back to 500 cal deficit. No debate.
The biology works against you at goal weight. Leptin drops during weight loss and doesn't fully recover. Ghrelin stays elevated for months. Your body will signal hunger at maintenance calories — this is physiological, not weakness. Knowing this in advance is your edge.

Maintenance Principles

Keep Tracking
MacroFactor Doesn't Stop
Switch to maintenance calories in the app. Keep logging. The scale and the app are your early warning system, not a diet tool. They're permanent infrastructure.
Reframe Exercise
From Loss to Preservation
At goal weight, exercise is no longer a weight loss tool. It is the maintenance mechanism. The psychology shifts — the routine doesn't. Same schedule, different purpose.
Retest
Function Health at Goal Weight
Bloodwork at goal weight confirms whether your new weight is sustainable or whether you've lost muscle. Testosterone, thyroid, and fasting insulin will tell you the truth.
The Weekly Ritual
Sunday Check-In Continues
Same 5-minute Sunday review. Forever. The moment you stop measuring is the moment the drift begins. You know this about yourself — keep the system that works.
Timeline reality check: 20 lbs in 4–5 months is realistic at your training volume and deficit. The first 5–7 lbs arrive fast (water/glycogen). The next 13 are slower. Muscle preservation — not scale speed — is the metric that matters most. A Withings scale reading 182 lbs with better body fat % than your 180 lb target is a win, not a failure.
